Negotiation Skills

By Mr.Shenal Rajakarunanayake

Today’s professional skills lecture about Negotiation Skills. below I write down some of details about today lesson. firstly we discuss about the main thing of the lecture it is what is Negotiation . Negotiation means simply talking with person for discuss their thoughts to get a common matching solution. Next our lecturer taught us about how we can engage in effective negotiation that process have several steps below I go through those important steps,

  • PREPARE THOROUGHLY
  • ESTABLISH CLEAR OBJECTIVES
  • NEGOTIATE EFFECTIVELY

PREPARE THOROUGHLY
In this step we want to research about the other party’s background, interest and any relevant information about their position. Also understanding industry and market conditions that might impact the negotiation. And also we have to set clear goals for negotiation. Lastly prepare a list of possible concessions and trade-offs we can make.Then our lecturer told us about in a negotiation our other party’s socioeconomic and educational background can significantly influence their negotiation style, approach, and effectiveness and there are some points to think about according to that those are ,

  • Cultural sensitivity
  • Economic literacy
  • Legal knowledge
  • Ethical considerations.


ESTABLISH CLEAR OBJECTIVES
In this step we want to Clearly define our goals and prioritize them. What are the "must haves" versus the "nice to haves"? Determine our walk away point or your "bottom line" to avoid making concessions that harm your interests. If we consider about more detailed information about above discussed things,

  • Prioritize our Goals : Determine which goals are most important and which ones you are willing to compromise on
  • Quantify When Possible : If applicable, quantify our objectives with specific numbers or metrics to make them more concrete.
  • Know our Walk Away Point: Be clear about the point at which you are willing to walk away from the negotiation. This is your "red line.


NEGOTIATE EFFECTIVELY
In this step we talked about different types of negotiations those are,

  • BATNA

    • - BATNA stands for "Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement." It refers to the course of action that a person can take if the current negotiation ends without reaching an agreement. In other words, it's the fall back option or Plan B that individuals or parties have in case the negotiation fails. Having a strong BATNA is crucial in negotiations, as it provides leverage and confidence to negotiate more effectively. Also there are some important steps in BATNA process those are ,

    • Step 1: Identify Your BATNA : Think about our current situation
    • Step 2: Evaluate Your BATNA : Consider other benefits.
    • Step 3: Assess Your Current Negotiation : Think about our needs clearly
    • Step 4: Use BATNA in the Negotiation.
    • Step 5: Negotiate Based on BATNA.



  • ZOPA

    • ZOPA stands for "Zone of Possible Agreement." It is the range in a negotiation within which a deal that satisfies both parties can be reached. In other words, it's the overlap between what the seller is willing to sell for (the lowest price they're willing to accept) and what the buyer is willing to pay (the highest price they're willing to pay). Finding the ZOPA is crucial for a successful negotiation because it indicates the potential for an agreement that both parties find acceptable. Also there are some important steps in BATNA process those are ,

    • Step 1: Identify Your Aspiration Range.
    • Step 2: Identify the Other Party's Aspiration Range.
    • Step 3: Determine the Overlapping Range.
    • Step 4: Prepare and Communicate Effectively
    • Step 5: Engage in Negotiation.
    • Step 6: Reach an Agreement.

Tips for business negotiation

Next our lecturer taught us some tips for business negotiation those are ,

1. Build Rapport

Start with Common Ground : Begin the negotiation by finding common interests or shared goals. This can create a sense of collaboration.
Be Genuine : Authenticity is key to building rapport. Be yourself and show a sincere interest in the other party.
Mirror Communication Styles : Adjust your communication style to match the other party's to establish a connection.


2.Active listening

Focus on the Speaker : Give your full attention to the other party. Put away distractions, such as phones or laptops.
Ask Open Ended Questions : Encourage the other party to elaborate on their thoughts and concerns.
Summarize and Clarify : Repeat what you've heard to ensure you understand correctly and to demonstrate your attentiveness.


3. stay calm and patient

Practice Relaxation Techniques : Deep breathing or meditation exercises can help you maintain composure.
Use Time Wisely : If you feel pressured, don't rush. Take your time to think through your responses.
Stay Focused on Objectives : Remember your goals and don't let emotional reactions derail the negotiation.


4. OFFER CREATIVE SOLUTIONS

Brainstorm Freely : Encourage open idea sharing without judgment. Sometimes, the best solutions come from unexpected places.
Think About Interests : Consider the underlying interests and needs of both parties when proposing solutions.
Be Flexible : Be willing to modify your proposals based on the other party's input.
