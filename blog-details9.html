Dining Etiquettes

Today’s professional skills lecture about Dining Etiquettes. This Is the formal way to dining table allocation. Below I write down some of details about today lesson.

  • dining rules
  • dining positions
  • types of business etiquettes

The above picture shows the formal allocation of dining table in that point our lecturer told us we must start dining from outside and work our way in to it. It means we must start dealing with our outside things like forks , knifes and spoons and then after charger and bowls and other things. Next we talked about rules for the napkins , firstly we place our napkin in our lap before we placing it we can fold it into large tringle or rectangle and also lecturer told us don’t crumple our napkin into a ball or wear our napkin as a bib. Then when we eating a bread, muffins or rolls, our lecturer told us to break off and eat one small piece at a time. If there is a community butter dish, take just enough for ourself and place it on our bread plate and do not want to continually dip our bread knife into the community butter dish. And also when we using Salt and pepper should never be separated from one another. Salt and pepper are like penguins—black and white and together for life. Even if an individual only asks for just one, always give them both.
After that our lecturer told us about basic and important manners we keepwhen dining those are


  • Take small bites , keep your mouth closed and finish chewing before continuing your conversation.
  • Try not to gulp your food , it isn't very attractive.
  • Do not blow on food that is hot. Wait until it cools down , or eat from the side of the bowl, when having soup.


Then after we talked about holding our silverware. There are only TWO correct ways to hold, and TWO ways to use our silverware. Using our knife and fork, practice the two different methods described below and determine which one is most comfortable. If you are a slow eater, then recommend the European method to speed up the process. If you are a fast eater, then recommend the American method to slow down the process. Those two methods are ,

  • PENCIL METHOD - Hold the utensil like you would a pencil. It should be resting between the tips of your pointer finger and middle finger with your thumb on top to hold it in place. The end of the utensil should be resting on the webbing of your hand.

  • SCALPEL METHOD - Hold the utensil like you would a surgeon's scalpel. The tines of the utensil should face downward. Your pointer finger will press on the back of the neck and the end of the handle should be touching the centre of your palm. Your remaining fingers grasp the utensil to hold it in place.


Next we talked about the different dining positions like,

  • CONTINENTAL/EUROPEAN STYLE
  • AMERICAN STYLE
  • FINISHED EATING






After that our lecturer told us about holding wine glasses if it is red wine glass we should hold at it bowl and if it is white wine glass we should hold at it bottom of glass.After that we talked about 5 types of business etiquettes those are ,
workplace etiquette.
Meal etiquette.
Communication etiquette.
Professionalism etiquette.
Meeting etiquette.


Then finally we talked about Role of first impression our lecturer told us some points under that those are ,

  • Genuine smile, not a fake one
  • Nice warm greeting
  • Exude positivity and energy
  • Appropriate eye contact
  • Active listening
  • Dress code
  • Grooming
